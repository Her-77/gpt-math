Unraveling the Mystery: Calculating the Volume of a Rectangular Solid in Water

22/02/2024

Have you ever wondered how to calculate the volume of an object without using conventional measuring tools? In this article, we will explore a fascinating method to find the volume of a rectangular solid submerged in water. This technique not only elucidates an interesting property of fluids but also demonstrates practical applications of basic principles in physics and mathematics.

The journey begins by understanding the concept of water displacement. When an object is submerged in water, it displaces a volume of water equal to its own volume. This principle is our golden ticket. For instance, when a rectangular solid is fully submerged in a cylindrical glass filled with water, the water level rises. By measuring this rise and knowing the cross-sectional area of the submerged object, we can calculate the volume of water displaced, which, in this case, equals the volume of the rectangular solid. Specifically, if the base area of the solid is 25 cm^2 and the water level rises by 4 cm, the volume of water displaced is $25 cm^2 imes 4 cm = 100 cm^3$.

However, the challenge doesn't end here. To fully grasp the total volume of the rectangular solid, we must also consider the scenario when the solid is partially submerged. The key here is to understand that the volume of water displaced when the solid is partially submerged gives us information about the part of the solid submerged. The difference in the water level when the solid is fully submerged versus when it's partially submerged reveals the total height of the solid.

Putting it all together, we calculate the volume of the rectangular solid using its base area and the total height (comprising both the submerged and the part above water). The equation that unravels this mystery is straightforward: Volume of the solid = base area $ imes$ total height. Given the base area of 25 cm^2 and the total height obtained from the change in water levels, we can find the volume of the rectangular solid.
