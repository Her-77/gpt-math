Unveiling the Infinity Norm of a Matrix Through Its Row Vectors

28/02/2024

In the fascinating world of linear algebra, norms play a pivotal role in measuring the size or length of vectors and matrices. Among the various norms, the infinity norm of a matrix, denoted as \\(||A||_{\infty}\\), stands out for its unique definition and significance. This article embarks on a journey to prove that the infinity norm of a matrix A equals the maximum 1-norm of its row vectors, bridging an essential connection between two seemingly disparate concepts.

To set the stage for our exploration, it's imperative to define the key players in our proof: the infinity norm (\\(||A||_{\infty}\\)) and the 1-norm of a row vector (\\(||a(i)||_1\\)). The infinity norm is calculated as the maximum absolute row sum of the matrix, \\(||A||_{\infty} = \max_{1 \leq i \leq m} \sum_{j}|a(i,j)|\\), while the 1-norm of a row vector is simply the sum of the absolute values of its elements, \\(||a(i)||_1 = \sum_{j}|a(i,j)|\\). Understanding these definitions is foundational for relating the matrix norm to its row vectors' norms.

Illustrating these concepts through an example can significantly enhance our comprehension. Consider the matrix A = [[1, -2], [-3, 4]]. By applying the definitions, we find that the infinity norm of A, \\(||A||_{\infty}\\), is 7, which is the maximum absolute row sum. Simultaneously, the 1-norms of the row vectors of A are {3, 7}, where the maximum 1-norm also equals 7. This example serves as a precursor to understanding the general proof.

Delving into the proof, we aim to demonstrate that \\(||A||_{\infty} = \max_{1 \leq i \leq m} ||a(i)||_1\\). The proof hinges on showing that the maximum absolute row sum of the matrix A is equivalent to the maximum of the 1-norms of its row vectors. This equivalence is not merely a coincidence but a fundamental property rooted in the definitions and elemental contributions of each component of the matrix to both norms.

To solidify our understanding, let's revisit the example matrix A = [[1, -2], [-3, 4]]. We've already established that \\(||A||_{\infty} = 7\\) and that the maximum 1-norm of its row vectors is also 7. This direct comparison between the infinity norm and the maximum 1-norm of the row vectors illustrates the proof's essence: the infinity norm of a matrix is, indeed, equal to the maximum 1-norm of its row vectors. Through logical reasoning and calculated steps, we unveil the intimate connection between these norms, enriching our grasp of linear algebra's nuances.
