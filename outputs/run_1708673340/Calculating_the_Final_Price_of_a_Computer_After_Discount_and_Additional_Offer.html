Calculating the Final Price of a Computer After Discount and Additional Offer

23/02/2024

When shopping for big-ticket items like computers, understanding how discounts affect the final price is crucial. In this case, we explore a scenario where a father is keen on purchasing a computer priced at 8000元 but wishes to secure an 80% discount, which is then intriguingly countered by the manager's unique offer. This situation provides a fascinating glimpse into the negotiations and calculations behind retail pricing.

To begin with, calculating the discounted price is the first step. An 80% discount does not mean paying 80% of the original price, but rather paying 20% of it, since the discount represents the portion of the price that is being reduced. However, in this scenario, the understanding is slightly misconceived, implying the father wants to pay 80% of the original price. Therefore, we calculate 80% of 8000元, which is achieved by multiplying the original price by 0.8 (80% in decimal form). This calculation gives us 6400元, which represents the price the father expects to pay initially.

However, the negotiation takes an interesting turn when the manager counters the father's proposed discount with an additional offer. Instead of granting the 80% discount, the manager proposes to add an extra 50元 to the discounted price. This move by the manager is a strategic one, slightly increasing the price while still making it seem like a good deal to the buyer.

To find the final price after this counteroffer, we need to adjust the previously calculated discounted price by adding the 50元. This means we take the 6400元, which represents an 80% of the original price, and add 50元 to it. Therefore, the final calculation is (0.8 * 8000) + 50, equating to 6450元. This figure represents the actual amount the father would need to pay to purchase the computer under the terms proposed by the manager.

This example not only demonstrates how to calculate discounts and adjust for additional offers but also highlights the importance of clear communication and understanding during price negotiations. It showcases the practical application of percentage calculations in real-life shopping scenarios, providing valuable insights for similar future encounters.
